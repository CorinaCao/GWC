What is an E-cigarette?

E-cigarettes are known by many different names such as e-cigs, e-hookahs, mods, vape pens, and vaporizers. Although e-cigarettes do not contain any tobacco, the Food and Drug Administration(FDA) classifies them as "tobacco" products. E-cigarettes come in a variety of shapes and sizes which is due to the size of the tank system or battery. They can look like cigarettes, cigars, pipes, pens, and USB flash drives. These devices operate by heating e-liquid(liquid nicotine) to a high enough temperature so that it produces an aerosal that is inhaled through the mouthpiece.

First Generation of E-cigarettes

This is one of the models that began the epidemic of e-cigarettes. These e-cigarettes were designed to imitate a tobacco cigarette having the end glow giving the user a sense of smoking a traditional cigarette. For this reason, this e-cigarette coined the name cig-alike. The three parts of this e-cigarette is a cartridge, an atomizer, and a battery. They are typically good for about 200-300 puffs and are disposable after its use.
Second Generation of E-cigarettes

Source
These devices are a more complex and elaborated versions of the first generation devices. The latest additions generate functions that are beneficial yet harmful to the user. The capacity of the cartridge or tank is increased and the amount of nicotine in each cartridge can be controlled by the user. This can be beneficial because they can choose an e-cigarette with the least amount of nicotine. Yet, the user can also choose to intake higher levels of nicotine. This can also be a danger hazard because the user has the option to intake high levels of nicotine which can develop many health risks.
Third Generation of E-Cigarettes and Beyond

Source
Eventually, the devices in the third generation of e-cigarettes become more powerful and efficient to use. The batteries come in a variety of shapes and sizes. The tank systems tend to be larger, meaning it requires less refilling. Out of all the devices from all generations, the devices in the third generation are usually preffered because users can customize their vaping experience by controlling features such as the battery power setting, the type of coil and wicking materials. In addition to this, the user can even manipulate the flow, thickness and volume of vapor produced.
Tobacco Cigarettes vs. E-cigarettes
Tobacco Cigarette E-cigarette
Tobacco is burned and inhaled as smoke. E-cigarettes heat up liquid nicotine to turn into odorless or vapor with flavoring.
Cigarettes can only be disposable. E-cigarettes can either be disposable or rechargeable.
One cigarette gives 12-15 puffs. One e-cigarette provides 500-600 puffs per cartridge (This equals about 55 cigarettes).
Generally contains between 8 and 20 mg of nictone. E-cigarettes come in multiple levels of nicotine ranging from 0-24mg.
Tobacco smoke given off by cigarettes contain around 7,000 chemicals. E-cigarettes release 31 harmful chemicals; some of these chemicals are also found in cigarette smoke.
